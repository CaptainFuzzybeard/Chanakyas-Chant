The game
Power in Indian politics has never belonged to the loudest voice. It has always belonged to the person who understood, one move earlier, where the room was going.

You have been reading this country for years. Every cabinet reshuffle, every budget speech, every minister who peaked too early or fell harder than anyone expected — you had a view before the headlines confirmed it. That knowledge has always lived in dinner table arguments and WhatsApp groups. It has never had a scoreboard.

Chanakya's Chant is that scoreboard. You form a political party — recruiting real Indian politicians — and build a 12-minister cabinet. Verified governance news scores them every week. You set a conviction level on each minister before Friday midnight — how confident you are they will rise. Sunday midnight, the table locks. The results are public. The argument is settled — for now.

Every conviction call leaves a record. At season end — triggered by the next general election — your calibration score, your archetype, and your full season history are crystallised into a permanent portrait. A record of how you read this period of Indian governance. Not a memory. A document.

How a season works
1
Form your party
543 real MPs. Every one of them with a record, a constituency, a reputation, and something to prove. You recruit politicians who will govern under your banner. No more than 3 from any single party. Your Political Capital decides who you can afford.
2
Build your government
Assign them to 12 ministry positions. Who runs Finance when the Budget is live. Who holds Home when the country is restless. Who sits at External Affairs when the region is burning. These are not abstract decisions. Indian governance makes them real every week.
3
Take a position, every week
Each week, before Friday midnight IST, you stake a position on every minister in your cabinet. Not a guess. A read. The news has been running all week — budget sessions, scams breaking at 11pm, alliances holding or fracturing. You decide who you back and how hard. That call is on record. That call has a consequence.
4
A season of consequence
Week after week, Indian politics produces events no one fully predicted. Budget sessions that move markets. Alliances that fracture overnight. Scams that surface at 11pm and change everything by morning. Through all of it, the table tracks who read it right — and who didn't. The season does not forgive sentimental picks or reactive decisions. It rewards the person who understood, one move earlier, where the room was going.
●
You carry the record forward
When a general election is called, the season closes and your record is crystallised. But the game does not end. A new season begins. The read continues. And now you know something about yourself as a political analyst that you didn’t know before.
How it works — complete reference
Political Capital
Every player starts with 1,000 Political Capital. Each politician has a cost — higher-scoring politicians cost more. You spend PC to build your roster. Costs move: when a politician scores well, their PC cost rises the following week. Spot them before they peak and you get more value for your capital. This is the hidden game within the game.
Party Cap
No more than 3 politicians from any single party in your roster. This is intentional — it prevents a party-loyalty strategy and forces genuine multi-party analysis. If BJP produces 6 strong performers in a week, you can only have 3 of them. The constraint is the game design.
Ministry Slots & Multipliers
Your cabinet has 12 slots. The PM slot multiplies your pick's score by ×1.5 — your single most important decision every week. Finance, Health, Infrastructure, and External Affairs are Anchor slots at ×1.2. All others are Base at ×1.0. Put a politician in their real-world ministry and they earn a hidden ×1.1 alignment bonus — most players never find it.
Conviction Levels
Each week, Mon–Fri, you set a conviction level on every cabinet minister. Certain (×1.4) — you're staking maximum confidence they'll rise. Believe (×1.2). Neutral (×1.0) — no call, always safe. Cautious (×0.9). Doubt (×0.7) — you expect them to fall. Certain and Doubt amplify both gains and losses. Neutral costs nothing and wins nothing. The conviction window slams shut Friday midnight IST. No exceptions.
Rank Lock
Every Sunday midnight IST, the week's news is processed, scored, and applied to every cabinet in the game. Conviction multipliers are applied. The leaderboard updates. This is called the rank lock — the moment when everything you did this week becomes a number next to your name. Between Monday and the following Sunday, you watch the table shift in real time as score events land.
Transfer Window
You get one free transfer per week — remove one politician from your roster and bring in another. Additional transfers each cost 4 points from your cabinet score. Unused free transfers do not carry over. If a politician defects to another party, you get a 72-hour emergency transfer window at no cost. The transfer window is the weekly heartbeat — it's what brings you back every Monday.
Score Sources & Tiers
Every score event is pulled from real governance news. T1 sources (PIB, official government releases) carry the most weight. T2 (The Hindu, Mint, Economic Times) carry moderate weight. T3 (regional outlets) carry lower weight and require corroboration. T4 (social and unverified) are almost never used. If a minister is mentioned in a headline, it has to meet source tier requirements before it scores. Nothing is assumed.
Calibration Score
Of all the conviction calls you made this season, what fraction pointed in the right direction? That fraction is your calibration score. 70% and above is elite — it means your gut consistently reads the political climate correctly. Below 50% means your instincts are systematically off. The calibration score is the skill signal that compounds over time. It cannot be faked, it cannot be gotten lucky at consistently. It is the closest thing this game has to a permanent reputation.
Politician Tags
Rising — the politician has strong positive momentum this week, their score is trending up. Set Certain and ride it. Scam — an active allegation is circling, their score is penalised by −15% until it resolves. High risk if they're in your cabinet. Syco — documented sycophancy bias has been flagged, their scores are volatile and tend to reflect party performance more than individual governance. Avoid Anchor slots for tagged politicians.
The Reckoning
When the season ends — triggered by the calling of a general election — your full season record is calculated and crystallised. Your archetype (Strategist, Contrarian, Consensus Player, Punter) is derived from your conviction pattern. Your calibration score is finalised. Your biggest win and biggest miss are named. Everything is rendered into a Season Portrait — a permanent, shareable record of how you read this period of Indian democracy. It does not reset. It does not disappear. It is the document you will look back on.
Coalition
Two players can form a coalition — their combined cabinet score is entered into a separate coalition leaderboard. It's a different kind of bet: you're not just backing your own reads, you're backing your partner's reads too. A coalition partner's strong week lifts you. Their bad week drags you. Choose carefully. You cannot be in a coalition with yourself, and the alliance must be mutually accepted.
Private Leagues
Any Pro subscriber can create a private league and invite up to 50 players with a code. Private leagues have their own leaderboard, separate from the national rankings. The banter is personal. The argument is between people who know each other. This is where "mujhe tujhse zyada maalum hai" actually lives — and where the real game is played.

Your archetype

Calls made
